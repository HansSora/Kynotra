Chest Exercises

Barbell Bench Press

Chest • Triceps • Front Delts

The king of chest exercises. Primary mass builder for the pectorals.

Compound Barbell

Instructions:

  1. Lie flat on bench, feet firmly on floor
  2. Grip bar slightly wider than shoulder width
  3. Unrack and hold above chest with arms extended
  4. Lower bar to mid-chest, elbows at 45-75°
  5. Press bar back up to starting position

Tips:

  • Retract shoulder blades
  • Keep wrists straight
  • Drive feet into floor for leg drive

Incline Bench Press

Upper Chest • Front Delts • Triceps

Targets the clavicular head of the pec. Essential for upper chest development.

Compound Barbell/Dumbbell

Instructions:

  1. Set bench to 30-45 degree incline
  2. Grip bar slightly wider than shoulders
  3. Lower bar to upper chest/collarbone area
  4. Press up and slightly back

Decline Bench Press

Lower Chest • Triceps

Emphasizes the lower pectorals. Allows for heavier loads than flat bench.

Compound Barbell

Instructions:

  1. Set bench to 15-30 degree decline
  2. Secure feet under pads
  3. Lower bar to lower chest
  4. Press up in a slight arc

Dumbbell Bench Press

Chest • Triceps • Front Delts

Greater range of motion than barbell. Excellent for muscle balance.

Compound Dumbbell

Instructions:

  1. Lie on bench with dumbbells at chest level
  2. Press dumbbells up, bringing them together at top
  3. Lower with control, allowing deep stretch

Dumbbell Flyes

Chest (stretch emphasis)

Isolation movement with maximum stretch. Great for chest development.

Isolation Dumbbell

Instructions:

  1. Lie on bench with arms extended above chest
  2. Lower dumbbells out to sides in arc motion
  3. Keep slight bend in elbows throughout
  4. Squeeze chest to bring weights back up

Cable Flyes

Chest (constant tension)

Constant tension throughout ROM. Excellent mind-muscle connection.

Isolation Cable

Instructions:

  1. Set cables to high, mid, or low position
  2. Step forward with slight forward lean
  3. Bring handles together in hugging motion
  4. Squeeze chest at contraction

Push-Ups

Chest • Triceps • Front Delts • Core

Fundamental bodyweight exercise. Endless variations for all levels.

Compound Bodyweight

Instructions:

  1. Hands slightly wider than shoulders
  2. Body in straight line from head to heels
  3. Lower chest to floor with elbows at 45°
  4. Push back up to starting position

Chest Dips

Lower Chest • Triceps • Front Delts

Compound movement emphasizing lower chest. Lean forward to target pecs.

Compound Bodyweight

Instructions:

  1. Grip parallel bars, arms extended
  2. Lean torso forward 30-45 degrees
  3. Lower until stretch in chest
  4. Push back up, maintaining lean

Machine Chest Press

Chest • Triceps

Fixed path for safety. Great for beginners or finishing sets.

Compound Machine

Instructions:

  1. Adjust seat so handles align with mid-chest
  2. Press handles forward until arms extended
  3. Lower with control

Pec Deck Machine

Chest (inner & outer)

Excellent isolation for chest squeeze. Constant tension throughout.

Isolation Machine

Instructions:

  1. Adjust seat and arm pads appropriately
  2. Place forearms against pads
  3. Squeeze arms together in front of chest
  4. Control the return to stretch

Back Exercises

Conventional Deadlift

Lower Back • Glutes • Hamstrings • Traps

The king of all exercises. Full posterior chain development.

Compound Barbell

Instructions:

  1. Stand with feet hip-width, bar over mid-foot
  2. Hinge and grip bar just outside legs
  3. Flatten back, chest up, shoulders over bar
  4. Drive through floor, extending hips and knees
  5. Lock out at top, reverse movement

Pull-Ups

Lats • Biceps • Rhomboids • Rear Delts

Essential vertical pull. Builds back width and V-taper.

Compound Bodyweight

Instructions:

  1. Grip bar slightly wider than shoulders, palms away
  2. Hang with arms fully extended
  3. Pull up until chin clears bar
  4. Lower with control to full extension

Chin-Ups

Lats • Biceps • Rhomboids

Supinated grip variation. More bicep involvement than pull-ups.

Compound Bodyweight

Instructions:

  1. Grip bar shoulder-width, palms facing you
  2. Hang with arms fully extended
  3. Pull up until chin clears bar
  4. Lower with control

Barbell Bent-Over Row

Lats • Rhomboids • Traps • Rear Delts

Primary back thickness builder. Heavy compound row.

Compound Barbell

Instructions:

  1. Hinge at hips, back flat, torso at 45°
  2. Grip bar just outside legs
  3. Pull bar to lower chest/upper abs
  4. Squeeze shoulder blades together
  5. Lower with control

Single-Arm Dumbbell Row

Lats • Rhomboids • Rear Delts

Unilateral row for balanced development. Great range of motion.

Compound Dumbbell

Instructions:

  1. Place one hand and knee on bench
  2. Keep back flat and parallel to floor
  3. Row dumbbell to hip, leading with elbow
  4. Squeeze lat at top, lower with control

Lat Pulldown

Lats • Biceps • Rhomboids

Machine alternative to pull-ups. Easier to control weight and form.

Compound Cable

Instructions:

  1. Grip bar wide, sit with thighs secured
  2. Lean back slightly, chest up
  3. Pull bar to upper chest
  4. Squeeze lats, control the return

Seated Cable Row

Lats • Rhomboids • Traps • Rear Delts

Controlled horizontal pull with constant tension.

Compound Cable

Instructions:

  1. Sit with feet on platforms, slight knee bend
  2. Grab handle, sit tall with extended arms
  3. Pull handle to lower chest/upper abs
  4. Squeeze shoulder blades, return with control

T-Bar Row

Lats • Rhomboids • Traps • Lower Back

Heavy compound row with stable base. Great for thickness.

Compound Barbell

Instructions:

  1. Straddle the bar, grip V-handle
  2. Hinge at hips, back flat
  3. Pull bar to chest, squeeze lats
  4. Lower with control

Face Pulls

Rear Delts • Rhomboids • Rotator Cuff

Essential for shoulder health and posture. High rep accessory.

Isolation Cable

Instructions:

  1. Set cable at face height with rope
  2. Grip rope with thumbs toward you
  3. Pull toward face, separating hands
  4. Externally rotate shoulders at end

Straight-Arm Pulldown

Lats • Teres Major

Lat isolation without bicep involvement. Great for mind-muscle connection.

Isolation Cable

Instructions:

  1. Stand facing cable with straight bar or rope
  2. Arms extended, slight lean forward
  3. Pull bar down to thighs keeping arms straight
  4. Squeeze lats at bottom, control return

Rack Pulls

Upper Back • Traps • Lower Back

Partial deadlift for upper back emphasis. Allows heavier loads.

Compound Barbell

Instructions:

  1. Set bar at knee height in rack
  2. Grip bar, flatten back
  3. Stand up with the weight
  4. Lower back to pins with control

Back Extensions (Hyperextensions)

Lower Back • Glutes • Hamstrings

Strengthens the spinal erectors. Essential for back health.

Isolation Bodyweight

Instructions:

  1. Position hips on pad, feet secured
  2. Lower torso toward floor
  3. Raise body until in line with legs
  4. Don't hyperextend past neutral

Shoulder Exercises

Barbell Overhead Press

Front Delts • Side Delts • Triceps

The primary shoulder compound. Builds pressing strength and mass.

Compound Barbell

Instructions:

  1. Grip bar just outside shoulders
  2. Unrack, bar at collarbone level
  3. Press overhead, moving head back then forward
  4. Lock out with bar over midfoot

Dumbbell Shoulder Press

Front Delts • Side Delts • Triceps

Greater ROM than barbell. Allows more natural movement pattern.

Compound Dumbbell

Instructions:

  1. Sit or stand with dumbbells at shoulder height
  2. Press dumbbells overhead
  3. Bring together at top, lower with control

Arnold Press

All Three Delt Heads

Rotational press hitting all delt heads. Named after Schwarzenegger.

Compound Dumbbell

Instructions:

  1. Start with dumbbells in front, palms facing you
  2. Rotate wrists while pressing up
  3. At top, palms face away
  4. Reverse motion on the way down

Dumbbell Lateral Raises

Side Deltoids

Essential for wide shoulders. Use controlled form, moderate weight.

Isolation Dumbbell

Instructions:

  1. Stand with dumbbells at sides
  2. Raise arms out to sides until parallel
  3. Lead with elbows, slight forward lean
  4. Lower with control

Front Raises

Front Deltoids

Isolation for front delts. Often not needed if pressing enough.

Isolation Dumbbell/Plate

Instructions:

  1. Hold weights in front of thighs
  2. Raise arms forward to shoulder height
  3. Keep slight bend in elbows
  4. Lower with control

Rear Delt Flyes

Rear Deltoids • Rhomboids

Critical for balanced shoulders and posture. Often neglected.

Isolation Dumbbell/Cable

Instructions:

  1. Bend at hips, torso nearly parallel to floor
  2. Hold dumbbells below chest
  3. Raise arms out to sides, leading with elbows
  4. Squeeze rear delts, lower with control

Cable Lateral Raises

Side Deltoids

Constant tension throughout ROM. Better resistance curve than dumbbells.

Isolation Cable

Instructions:

  1. Stand sideways to low cable
  2. Grab handle with far hand
  3. Raise arm out to side until parallel
  4. Lower with control

Upright Rows

Side Delts • Traps • Front Delts

Compound shoulder/trap movement. Use wide grip for shoulder safety.

Compound Barbell/Dumbbell

Instructions:

  1. Grip bar/dumbbells with wide grip
  2. Pull up along body, leading with elbows
  3. Stop when elbows reach shoulder height
  4. Lower with control

Barbell/Dumbbell Shrugs

Upper Traps

Direct trap isolation. Go heavy with controlled form.

Isolation Barbell/Dumbbell

Instructions:

  1. Hold weight with arms at sides
  2. Shrug shoulders straight up toward ears
  3. Squeeze at top for 1-2 seconds
  4. Lower slowly

Leg Exercises

Barbell Back Squat

Quads • Glutes • Hamstrings • Core

The king of leg exercises. Builds overall lower body strength and size.

Compound Barbell

Instructions:

  1. Bar on upper traps (high bar) or rear delts (low bar)
  2. Feet shoulder-width, toes slightly out
  3. Descend by pushing hips back and bending knees
  4. Go below parallel (hip below knee)
  5. Drive through heels to stand

Front Squat

Quads • Core • Upper Back

More quad-dominant than back squat. Requires good mobility.

Compound Barbell

Instructions:

  1. Bar on front delts, clean grip or crossed arms
  2. Keep elbows high throughout
  3. Descend with upright torso
  4. Drive up maintaining elbow position

Romanian Deadlift

Hamstrings • Glutes • Lower Back

Hip-hinge movement targeting posterior chain with constant tension.

Compound Barbell/Dumbbell

Instructions:

  1. Hold bar/dumbbells in front of thighs
  2. Push hips back, lowering weight along legs
  3. Keep slight knee bend, back flat
  4. Lower until hamstring stretch, rise by driving hips

Leg Press

Quads • Glutes • Hamstrings

Machine compound movement. Allows heavy loading with less spinal stress.

Compound Machine

Instructions:

  1. Sit with back flat against pad
  2. Feet shoulder-width on platform
  3. Lower until knees at 90 degrees
  4. Press through heels to extend legs

Hack Squat

Quads • Glutes

Machine squat variation. More quad-focused with stable back position.

Compound Machine

Instructions:

  1. Position shoulders under pads
  2. Feet shoulder-width on platform
  3. Lower by bending knees
  4. Press through feet to stand

Walking Lunges

Quads • Glutes • Hamstrings

Unilateral exercise for leg development and balance.

Compound Bodyweight/Dumbbell

Instructions:

  1. Step forward into lunge position
  2. Lower until both knees at 90 degrees
  3. Push off front foot to step through
  4. Alternate legs with each step

Bulgarian Split Squat

Quads • Glutes • Hamstrings

Single-leg squat with rear foot elevated. Excellent for strength and balance.

Compound Dumbbell/Bodyweight

Instructions:

  1. Place rear foot on bench behind you
  2. Front foot about 2 feet ahead
  3. Descend until rear knee nearly touches floor
  4. Drive through front heel to stand

Leg Extension

Quadriceps

Quad isolation machine. Great for building the teardrop.

Isolation Machine

Instructions:

  1. Sit with back against pad
  2. Pad above ankles, below shins
  3. Extend legs until straight
  4. Squeeze quads, lower with control

Lying Leg Curl

Hamstrings

Hamstring isolation. Essential for balanced leg development.

Isolation Machine

Instructions:

  1. Lie face down, pad above heels
  2. Hips pressed against pad
  3. Curl heels toward glutes
  4. Squeeze hamstrings, lower with control

Barbell Hip Thrust

Glutes • Hamstrings

Best exercise for glute development. High muscle activation.

Compound Barbell

Instructions:

  1. Upper back on bench, feet flat on floor
  2. Barbell across hips with pad
  3. Drive hips up until body parallel to floor
  4. Squeeze glutes at top, lower with control

Standing Calf Raises

Gastrocnemius

Primary calf builder targeting the larger gastrocnemius.

Isolation Machine

Instructions:

  1. Balls of feet on platform
  2. Lower heels below platform level
  3. Rise onto toes as high as possible
  4. Squeeze at top, lower with control

Seated Calf Raises

Soleus

Targets the soleus muscle (deep calf). Important for complete calf development.

Isolation Machine

Instructions:

  1. Sit with pad over knees
  2. Balls of feet on platform
  3. Lower heels, then rise onto toes
  4. Full ROM, controlled tempo

Arm Exercises

Barbell Bicep Curl

Biceps • Forearms

Classic bicep builder. Allows heavy loading for mass.

Isolation Barbell

Instructions:

  1. Stand with shoulder-width grip
  2. Keep elbows pinned to sides
  3. Curl bar toward shoulders
  4. Lower with control

Alternating Dumbbell Curl

Biceps

Allows supination for peak bicep contraction.

Isolation Dumbbell

Instructions:

  1. Hold dumbbells at sides, palms in
  2. Curl one arm, rotating palm up
  3. Squeeze at top, lower with control
  4. Alternate arms

Hammer Curls

Biceps • Brachialis • Forearms

Neutral grip hits brachialis for arm thickness.

Isolation Dumbbell

Instructions:

  1. Hold dumbbells with neutral grip
  2. Keep palms facing each other throughout
  3. Curl toward shoulders
  4. Lower with control

Preacher Curl

Biceps (long head emphasis)

Eliminates cheating. Great for bicep peak development.

Isolation Barbell/Dumbbell

Instructions:

  1. Sit at preacher bench, arms over pad
  2. Grip bar, arms nearly extended
  3. Curl bar toward shoulders
  4. Lower with control, don't fully extend

Cable Bicep Curl

Biceps

Constant tension throughout ROM. Great for pump.

Isolation Cable

Instructions:

  1. Stand facing low cable with bar/rope
  2. Keep elbows at sides
  3. Curl handle toward shoulders
  4. Lower with control

Incline Dumbbell Curl

Biceps (long head stretch)

Long head emphasis through stretched position.

Isolation Dumbbell

Instructions:

  1. Sit on 45-60 degree incline bench
  2. Let arms hang straight down
  3. Curl dumbbells up
  4. Lower fully to stretch biceps

Close-Grip Bench Press

Triceps • Chest • Front Delts

Compound tricep builder. Allows heavy loading.

Compound Barbell

Instructions:

  1. Grip bar shoulder-width or slightly narrower
  2. Lower bar to lower chest
  3. Keep elbows close to body
  4. Press up, focusing on triceps

Tricep Rope Pushdowns

Triceps (all 3 heads)

Effective isolation with constant tension.

Isolation Cable

Instructions:

  1. Stand facing high cable with rope
  2. Elbows pinned to sides
  3. Push rope down until arms extended
  4. Spread rope at bottom for contraction

Skull Crushers (Lying Tricep Ext.)

Triceps (long head emphasis)

Targets long head with stretch. Classic tricep builder.

Isolation Barbell/EZ Bar

Instructions:

  1. Lie on bench, arms extended above chest
  2. Lower bar toward forehead by bending elbows
  3. Keep upper arms stationary
  4. Extend arms to starting position

Overhead Tricep Extension

Triceps (long head)

Full stretch on long head. Great for mass.

Isolation Dumbbell/Cable

Instructions:

  1. Hold weight overhead with both hands
  2. Lower behind head by bending elbows
  3. Keep upper arms close to ears
  4. Extend to starting position

Tricep Dips

Triceps • Chest • Front Delts

Compound bodyweight tricep builder. Keep torso upright.

Compound Bodyweight

Instructions:

  1. Grip bars, arms extended, body upright
  2. Lower by bending elbows behind you
  3. Go until upper arms parallel
  4. Push back up to start

Wrist Curls

Forearm Flexors

Forearm isolation for grip strength and size.

Isolation Barbell/Dumbbell

Instructions:

  1. Rest forearms on bench, wrists hanging off
  2. Hold weight with palms up
  3. Curl weight up using only wrists
  4. Lower with control

Core Exercises

Plank

Rectus Abs • Obliques • Transverse

Foundational isometric core exercise. Builds stability.

Isometric Bodyweight

Instructions:

  1. Forearms on floor, elbows under shoulders
  2. Body in straight line from head to heels
  3. Brace core, squeeze glutes
  4. Hold for prescribed time

Crunches

Upper Rectus Abdominis

Basic ab exercise. Focus on spinal flexion, not hip flexion.

Isolation Bodyweight

Instructions:

  1. Lie on back, knees bent, feet flat
  2. Hands behind head or crossed on chest
  3. Lift shoulders off floor by contracting abs
  4. Lower with control, don't fully relax

Hanging Leg Raises

Lower Abs • Hip Flexors

Advanced lower ab exercise. Great for definition.

Isolation Bodyweight

Instructions:

  1. Hang from bar with arms extended
  2. Raise legs until parallel to floor (or higher)
  3. Keep legs straight or slightly bent
  4. Lower with control, no swinging

Russian Twists

Obliques • Rectus Abs

Rotational core exercise targeting obliques.

Isolation Bodyweight/Weight

Instructions:

  1. Sit with knees bent, lean back slightly
  2. Hold weight or clasp hands
  3. Rotate torso side to side
  4. Keep core braced throughout

Cable Woodchops

Obliques • Core Rotators

Functional rotational movement with resistance.

Isolation Cable

Instructions:

  1. Set cable high, stand sideways
  2. Grip handle with both hands
  3. Pull diagonally across body to hip
  4. Control the return, repeat, switch sides

Dead Bug

Transverse Abs • Core Stability

Anti-extension exercise. Great for core control.

Anti-Extension Bodyweight

Instructions:

  1. Lie on back, arms up, knees at 90°
  2. Press lower back into floor
  3. Lower opposite arm and leg toward floor
  4. Return to start, alternate sides

Ab Wheel Rollout

Rectus Abs • Obliques • Lats

Advanced anti-extension exercise. Very challenging.

Anti-Extension Ab Wheel

Instructions:

  1. Kneel on floor, grip ab wheel
  2. Roll out as far as you can control
  3. Keep core tight, don't arch lower back
  4. Contract abs to roll back

Mountain Climbers

Core • Hip Flexors • Cardio

Dynamic core exercise with cardio element.

Dynamic Bodyweight

Instructions:

  1. Start in push-up position
  2. Drive one knee toward chest
  3. Quickly switch legs
  4. Keep hips low, core engaged

Bicycle Crunches

Obliques • Rectus Abs

Rotational crunch hitting all ab muscles.

Isolation Bodyweight

Instructions:

  1. Lie on back, hands behind head
  2. Lift shoulders and legs off floor
  3. Bring elbow to opposite knee
  4. Alternate sides in cycling motion

Pallof Press

Obliques • Core Anti-Rotation

Anti-rotation core exercise. Excellent for core stability.

Anti-Rotation Cable/Band

Instructions:

  1. Stand sideways to cable at chest height
  2. Hold handle at chest with both hands
  3. Press handle straight out
  4. Resist rotation, return to chest

Reverse Crunch

Lower Abs

Targets lower abs by lifting hips off floor.

Isolation Bodyweight

Instructions:

  1. Lie on back, legs up, knees bent
  2. Curl hips off floor toward chest
  3. Don't use momentum, control movement
  4. Lower hips with control

Side Plank

Obliques • Core Stability

Lateral core stability. Targets obliques isometrically.

Isometric Bodyweight

Instructions:

  1. Lie on side, forearm on floor under shoulder
  2. Lift hips off floor
  3. Body in straight line from head to feet
  4. Hold for time, switch sides

Gym Terminology

Essential fitness terms every lifter should know

Reps (Repetitions)

One complete movement of an exercise. Example: lowering and pressing a barbell once = 1 rep.

Sets

A group of consecutive reps. Example: 3 sets of 10 reps = performing 10 reps, resting, then repeating 2 more times.

Progressive Overload

Gradually increasing the weight, reps, or volume over time to continuously challenge muscles. The #1 principle for growth.

Hypertrophy

The increase in muscle size through training. Typically achieved with 8-12 reps, moderate weight, and high volume.

Compound Exercise

An exercise that works multiple muscle groups simultaneously. Examples: squat, bench press, deadlift, overhead press.

Isolation Exercise

An exercise targeting a single muscle group. Examples: bicep curl, lateral raise, leg extension.

Superset

Performing two exercises back-to-back without rest. Can target same or opposing muscle groups for efficiency.

Drop Set

Performing a set to failure, then immediately reducing weight and continuing. Great for muscle fatigue and pump.

1RM (One Rep Max)

The maximum weight you can lift for one repetition. Used to calculate training percentages.

Time Under Tension (TUT)

The total time a muscle is under strain during a set. Slower reps = more TUT = more muscle stimulus.

RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion)

A 1-10 scale measuring effort level. RPE 8 = you could do 2 more reps. RPE 10 = absolute failure.

DOMS (Delayed Onset Muscle Soreness)

Muscle soreness appearing 24-72 hours after training. Normal and indicates muscle damage/repair cycle.
