Barbell Bench Press

Chest • Triceps • Front Delts

The king of chest exercises. Compound movement targeting pectorals with heavy load potential.

Compound Barbell

Step-by-Step Instructions:

  1. Lie flat on a bench, feet firmly on the floor
  2. Grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder width
  3. Unrack the bar and hold it above your chest with arms extended
  4. Lower the bar slowly to mid-chest, elbows at ~75° angle
  5. Press the bar back up to the starting position
  6. Lock out arms at the top without hyperextending elbows

Tips:

  • Retract your shoulder blades and arch your upper back slightly
  • Drive your feet into the floor for leg drive
  • Keep your wrists straight — don't bend them back
  • Breathe in on the way down, exhale on the press

Common Mistakes:

  • Bouncing the bar off the chest
  • Flaring elbows to 90° (injury risk)
  • Lifting hips off the bench
  • Not using a spotter for heavy sets

Barbell Back Squat

Quads • Glutes • Hamstrings • Core

The most effective lower body exercise. Works nearly every muscle in the body.

Compound Barbell

Instructions:

  1. Position bar on upper traps (high bar) or rear delts (low bar)
  2. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly out
  3. Brace your core and unrack the bar
  4. Descend by pushing hips back and bending knees
  5. Go below parallel (hip crease below knee)
  6. Drive through heels to stand back up

Common Mistakes:

  • Knees caving inward (valgus)
  • Not hitting proper depth
  • Rounding the lower back
  • Rising on toes instead of driving through heels

Conventional Deadlift

Back • Glutes • Hamstrings • Traps

The ultimate full-body strength builder. Targets the entire posterior chain.

Compound Barbell

Instructions:

  1. Stand with feet hip-width apart, bar over mid-foot
  2. Hinge at hips, grip bar just outside knees
  3. Flatten back, chest up, shoulders over or slightly in front of bar
  4. Drive through the floor, extending hips and knees simultaneously
  5. Lock out at top with hips fully extended
  6. Lower bar by hinging hips first, then bending knees

Common Mistakes:

  • Rounding the lower back
  • Starting with hips too low (it's not a squat)
  • Bar drifting away from the body
  • Hyperextending at the top

Gym Terminology

Essential fitness terms every lifter should know

Reps (Repetitions)

One complete movement of an exercise. Example: lowering and pressing a barbell once = 1 rep.

Sets

A group of consecutive reps. Example: 3 sets of 10 reps = performing 10 reps, resting, then repeating 2 more times.

Progressive Overload

Gradually increasing the weight, reps, or volume over time to continuously challenge muscles. The #1 principle for growth.

Hypertrophy

The increase in muscle size through training. Typically achieved with 8-12 reps, moderate weight, and high volume.

Compound Exercise

An exercise that works multiple muscle groups simultaneously. Examples: squat, bench press, deadlift, overhead press.

Isolation Exercise

An exercise targeting a single muscle group. Examples: bicep curl, lateral raise, leg extension.

Superset

Performing two exercises back-to-back without rest. Can target same or opposing muscle groups for efficiency.

Drop Set

Performing a set to failure, then immediately reducing weight and continuing. Great for muscle fatigue and pump.

1RM (One Rep Max)

The maximum weight you can lift for one repetition. Used to calculate training percentages.

Time Under Tension (TUT)

The total time a muscle is under strain during a set. Slower reps = more TUT = more muscle stimulus.

RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion)

A 1-10 scale measuring effort level. RPE 8 = you could do 2 more reps. RPE 10 = absolute failure.

DOMS (Delayed Onset Muscle Soreness)

Muscle soreness appearing 24-72 hours after training. Normal and indicates muscle damage/repair cycle.
